LGBTQ+ Affirming Therapy: A Space to Be Fully Yourself

By Hila Teller, LCSW · Licensed Clinical Social Worker · Los Altos, CA

Finding the right therapist is important for anyone — but for LGBTQ+ individuals, it matters in a particular way. You need a therapist who doesn't just tolerate your identity, but affirms it. Someone who understands the unique stressors that come with being queer or trans in the world, without making your identity the "problem" to be solved.

My practice in Los Altos, CA is a fully affirming, judgment-free space for LGBTQ+ adults.

Unique Challenges LGBTQ+ Adults May Face

  • Minority stress — the chronic stress of navigating a world that isn't always safe
  • Family rejection or complicated coming-out experiences
  • Internalized homophobia or transphobia
  • Grief related to lost relationships or missed milestones
  • Identity exploration and questions of self-acceptance
  • Trauma from discrimination, harassment, or violence
  • Relationship dynamics unique to LGBTQ+ partnerships

These experiences don't exist in isolation. They layer on top of whatever else you're carrying — depression, anxiety, trauma, relationship struggles. Good therapy holds all of it.

What Affirming Therapy Looks Like

Affirming therapy means your identity is treated as a given — not a topic for debate or a lens through which all your problems are filtered. We focus on you: your history, your feelings, your goals, your growth.

If your identity is something you want to explore or discuss, we absolutely can. But you won't be pushed, pathologized, or misunderstood. This is your space.

Why the Therapeutic Relationship Matters Especially Here

For many LGBTQ+ clients, finding safety in a relationship — particularly with an authority figure — hasn't always been easy. Building that trust is something we do deliberately and carefully. When it works, the therapeutic relationship itself becomes a model for what genuine acceptance and connection can feel like.
